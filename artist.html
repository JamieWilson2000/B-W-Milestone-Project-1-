in interview

meet Sophie Loizou on the launch of her third album "Untold"

tmn : Hi Sophie, lets start off with your musical background!
SL : "I was about 19 when I started to compose music, while studying fine art. I'd been listening to electronic music whilst working on my art. I realised that what I was hearing in sonic textures and gestures translated across to my mark-making. This was what sparked my interest in working with sound. I started off with a cheap four-track and my voice, and then maybe a year later I bought my first computer and a Korg X5 synth."

tmn : so tell us, how did you get into production?
SL : "Well after taking a break from composing, everything had changed because of the developments in computing. It was really exciting to find so many new ideas and approaches to making music. Between 2011 and 2014 I explored a lot of new computer based processes. I started experimenting with MAX and Reaktor, and open-source software like Spear, Hack and Cecillia. Being able to change the dynamic shape of recorded sound with a gesture became a real game changer."
tmn : so how does a track come together?
SL : "I start by capturing or creating an environment from field recordings, chunks of designed sound, or randomly generated granular sounds. I draw out the harmonic basis of a piece from the sounds, building tonal structures out of the frequency content, which involves a degree of tuning non-musical materials. Once the overall shape is formed I start working on the structure of the track where the pieces begin to take on a life of their own creating wild and unpredictable dynamics."
tmn : tell us about your latest release
SL : "I've signed to Houndstooth who are releasing Untold. It's an LP, a collection of poems with accompanying audio and artworks, an AV show, a multimedia work and a lecture performance. The sounds and images developed for the planned tour are drawn from the alien life forms and landscapes that inspired the album"
